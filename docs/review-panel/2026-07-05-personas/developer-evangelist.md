## Developer Evangelist Critique — "Borrow Native"

**1. The name is the single strongest asset here, and it's underexploited.** "Borrow Native" does real work: it puns on the borrow checker (Rust's signature pain point) and "native"/agent-native in one shot, and it's short enough to say out loud. It beats the other candidates listed in the gremlin doc ("Fearless Loop," "Rust, Verified," "Compile or Concede" — the last is actually punchier as a tagline than a name). But nowhere in workshop-design.md is the name itself *unpacked* for a newcomer — there's no line that says "you'll become a borrow-checker native, not just someone who eventually gets past it." That's a free hook currently left on the table.

**2. The pitch line buries the lede.** The one-line pitch — "Learn Rust the agent-native way: agent-literate practitioners work through harness-driven exercises with the compiler and test suite as a deterministic gate, and Coachgremlin grading the conceptual/idiomatic layer on top" — is a spec sentence, not a hook. It front-loads "agent-literate practitioners" (jargon that reads as gatekeeping) before it tells you the actual promise. The better line is already sitting one paragraph later, unused as the headline: *"learn Rust the way you already work, with your agent, and let the compiler — not an opinion — be the first gate."* That sentence is the ten-second hook; the current draft pitch is not.

**3. No "why now" or social proof beat.** There's no acknowledgment that this is timely (agents + systems languages is a live conversation right now) — nothing that would make me, sharing this on a timeline, add my own one-liner of context. The differentiator paragraph is honest and well-reasoned but reads like a design memo ("Borrow Native's bet is that layering agent-native delivery... is a genuine addition, not a rebuild") — that's internal justification language, not shareable copy.

**4. Friction point, not a content problem but a first-impression one:** the README is literally the scaffold template right now (Implementation Status: "Scaffolded 2026-07-05"). If someone clicks through today, first ten seconds is a broken promise. Not counted against the concept, but flagging since it's the actual current state of the hook's delivery vehicle.

No other findings — the naming/differentiator substance is solid; my notes are about surfacing it, not fixing it.
